How Much Water to Drink

A common rule of thumb is 8 glasses (about 2 litres) per day, but individual needs vary with body size, activity level, climate, and diet. A simple test: urine should be pale yellow, not dark. Most fruits, vegetables, and other beverages count toward total fluid intake. Increase water during exercise, hot weather, or illness with fever. You usually don't need to drink before you feel thirsty.
